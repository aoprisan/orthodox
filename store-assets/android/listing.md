## Full description

```
Orthodox Calendar is a quiet, hand-rolled liturgical calendar for the
Christian Orthodox Church — feasts of the Lord and the Theotokos, the
commemorations of the saints, the fasting rules of the typikon, and the
full Pascha-derived moveable cycle. Open it, and the day stands before
you the way it stands in the calendar of the Church.

ɪᴛᴡᴏ ᴄᴀʟᴇɴᴅᴀʀs, ᴏɴᴇ ᴀᴘᴘ
A single toggle moves the whole calendar between the Revised Julian
(New) calendar used in most autocephalous churches and the Julian (Old)
calendar still observed by the Russian, Serbian, Georgian, Jerusalem and
Athonite Patriarchates and by many traditional parishes. Pascha is
shared by both — computed via Meeus's Julian algorithm — and pulls the
full Pascha cycle behind it: Triodion, Great Lent, Holy Week, the
Pentecostarion, and the apostles' fast.

ғᴇᴀsᴛs ᴀɴᴅ sᴀɪɴᴛs
Twelve Great Feasts. The fixed sanctoral cycle, with the principal
saints of each day named in both English and Romanian. The lesser
commemorations of the extended synaxarion, drawn from the standard
Orthodox menologion. And for the days that carry them, the prose Lives
of the Saints — viețile sfinților — load on demand in Romanian, so the
app stays light when you don't need them and rich when you do.

ғᴀsᴛɪɴɢ
Every day shows its fasting rule at a glance — strict, wine-and-oil,
fish, dairy, or fast-free — following the typical Byzantine typikon.
Saturday and Sunday relaxations during Great Lent are honoured, fish on
the Annunciation and Palm Sunday, the fast-free week after Pascha and
the days between Christmas and Theophany.

ʙɪʟɪɴɢᴜᴀʟ
English and Romanian, with one tap to switch. Saint names, feast titles,
and the Lives prose are all localised — the calendar that an English-
speaking Orthodox Christian and a Romanian-speaking one both want, in
one place.

ᴀ ʙʏᴢᴀɴᴛɪɴᴇ ᴜɪ
Porphyry and gold leaf, parchment and meander borders, illuminated drop
caps, candle-light shadows. The visual language of an icon panel rather
than a stock SaaS calendar. Made to be opened daily without growing
tired.

ᴘʀɪᴠᴀᴄʏ
No analytics. No ads. No tracking. No accounts. No login. No
permissions. The app collects nothing, transmits nothing, and works
fully offline. The supplementary Lives prose is fetched on demand from
the app's own static origin; everything else ships inside the app.

Free, open source under the calendar's GitHub repository, and built for
people who pray the Church's year and want it close to hand.
```
